Professional Work

I've been a developer all over the place, from the energy sector to a philanthropic sports equipment supplier. For specifics about my work experience, take a look below at some of my accomplishments.

If you'd prefer a more minimalist, nitty-gritty overview, check out my resume or my LinkedIn.

IMC Financial Markets is a high-frequency/algorithmic trading firm based out of Amsterdam. I had the opportunity to intern at IMC in the summer of 2017 as a software engineer in their Chicago office, and my project was to create a trades analysis tool in Java that leveraged Spark and HDFS.

The intention of my project was to accurately and efficiently process terabytes of trade data a day to provide concrete reasons as to why IMC might have missed out on profitable opportunities in the market. By the end of the summer, my intern team and I made significant enough progress that the tool was put into production to be used by traders on a daily basis.

Nisa Investment Advisors is an investment manager based in St. Louis that primarily serves institutional investors. I interned with Nisa during the fall of 2016, and filled a fairly general software IT role.

My first project was to create an OData service to parse data from an SQL Server view and display the data to a web endpoint, which was implemented primarily in C# and made use of the OData RESTful API.

For the remainder of the semester, I architechted a proof-of-concept version zero of an automated testing framework to routinely test Nisa's proprietary web applications. I wrote the TestComplete test suite in a built-in language called TestComplete JScript, and implemented the surrounding software that automated and recorded the tests using C# and MySQL.

I interned with Visa in Austin the summer of 2016 as a Switching Systems and Network Processing Intern, working specifically on their payment processing backend systems.

My biggest project that summer was optimizing and benchmarking Visa standard library functions in C++ to reduce overhead, which resulted in a 2.6% improvement in system performance. Although a 2.6% change may not seem that drastic, the improved processing speed will save Visa $1,000,000 in rented black box space every three year cycle.

A good friend of mine wanted to use the world's love for soccer to fuel altruism, and from his vision Kizazi was born. For every soccer ball bought, Kizazi donates a portion of their net profits to Kiva to fund microloans for aspiring entrepreneurs in developing countries.

I worked for Kizazi the summer of 2015 as their sole engineer, and my main focus was creating their website. Check out what we accomplished by clicking the landing page below.

Frontier Utilities is Texas-based energy provider. I was lucky enough to work there the summer before I started college as a data analysis/IT intern. This was my first "real job", and much to my surprise I actually got to do things other than make coffee and move stacks of paper from point A to point B.

Instead, I was mentored directly by the company's CIO and worked on a variety of projects as they came up. I used MySQL and Excel to create in-depth reports on customer and company activity, such as profits for a zipcode or client retention during a given month. On top of that, I wrote a few SQL stored procedures to update underlying data for reports that were then fed to users through an internal web app.

Peer Education

I like helping people learn stuff, and the sections below highlight some of the ways I've tried to give back to the community as an educator.

As I noted before, I'll be working as a middle school math teacher through Teach For America for the next two years, but after that I'm looking into ed-tech as a way to combine my passion for peer improvement with my love for software development.

I was a Computer Science TA for the introductory Java class, CSE131, during my sophomore and junior years in college. I specifically stayed with the introductory class because I felt that it gave me the best opportunity to help younger students get a solid foundation in CS before moving on to tougher classes.

Additionally during that time, I served as an Engineering Tutor, which gave me the opportunity to work one-on-one with struggling students to help them with some of the tougher concepts.

I'm a member of an on-campus student group called LIVE (Leaders in Interpersonal Violence Education). LIVE provides facilitations regarding topics such as sexual assault and toxic masculinity with the goal of creating a safe, productive environment for some tough -- but necessary -- conversations.

As a trained LIVE facilitator, I am in charge of giving presentations and guiding discussions for other student groups, class sections, and sports teams on campus.

While in high school, I tutored two middle schoolers in Spanish and algebra. These weekly sessions were my first real experience in peer education, and it's been a lot of fun going home during breaks from college and seeing the kids I tutored grow up to become successful in high school and beyond.

I was also the assistant coach at my high school's summer soccer camp, herding kids from 5-12 years old and (hopefully) improving their technical skills along the way.
