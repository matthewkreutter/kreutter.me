Peer Education

I like helping people learn stuff. In high school, I tutored two middle schoolers in Spanish and algebra, which was my first real experience in "teaching". Additionally, I was the assistant coach at my school's summer soccer camp, herding kids from 5-12 years old and (hopefully) improving their technical skills along the way.

In college, I was a Computer Science TA for the introductory Java class, CSE131, for sophomore and junior year. I specifically stayed with the introductory class because I felt that it gave me the best opportunity to help younger devs get a solid foundation in CS before moving on to tougher classes.

Last but certainly not least, I was a member of an on-campus student group called LIVE (Leaders in Interpersonal Violence Education). LIVE provides facilitations regarding topics such as sexual assault and toxic masculinity with the goal of creating a safe, productive environment for some tough -- but necessary -- conversations. As a trained LIVE facilitator, I was in charge of giving presentations and guiding discussions for other student groups, class sections, or sports teams on campus.
